General Practice Teams

Relating to quality, effectiveness and human resource costs: a mixed methods study in England


General practice is one of the cornerstones of the National Health Service but is currently under pressure as a result of a shortage of the workforce and an increasing number of people with long-term conditions who need regular care from General Practice and primary care. These pressures mean that it is essential to maximise the efficient organisation of staff teams in General Practice.

There is very little evidence to inform GPs and service commissioners what size or structure of practice, or what mix of professional staff, works best for team members and patients. Therefore, this research aims to provide evidence as to how the composition of GP teams and team climate affect the quality of care and health outcomes for patients, and the costs for practice.

Research Aim: To explore how organisation, skill mix and climate in GP teams relate to quality of care, clinical effectiveness and human resource costs.

In this mixed methods research study, we are carrying out the following:

  • A literature review to compile lessons about skills mix in similar contexts.
  • Analysis of currently available data sets to assess how organisation and skill mix in practices in England are related to the quality and effectiveness of care, based on hospitalisation for conditions which should be managed within General Practice.
  • Survey staff in a sample of practices nationwide to explore how workforce issues affect staff wellbeing and job satisfaction.
  • Observe and interview staff in 12 selected practices to investigate how team climate affects patient experiences.
  • Create guidelines for all practices on the best mix of professional staff and promoting productive team climate through consensus.
  • Use innovative means to disseminate the findings throughout the GP community, and to NHS managers, government organisations, academics and the public.

This research is funded by the NIHR Health Services and Delivery Research programme (Project number 17/08/34, Reference number 152125) and led by University of Surrey.

The Professionals and Commissioners Panel

Nine members working together to make an impact for general practice in urban and rural areas in England.

We are inviting commissioners, GP Partners, GP Nurses, Pharmacists, Managers and Allied Health Professionals including physiotherapists to join the Professionals and Commissioners Panel (PCP) for three online sessions held over 18 months from January 2019 to June 2020.

These meetings will be an opportunity to support the mixed methods research teams to

  • capture the context, relevance, and sense checking resources and materials for the research project
  • help us explore the burden being placed on participants, any barriers to participants taking part
  • inform the early stages of developing implementation guidelines and dissemination for national, regional, local stakeholders including General Practice teams

In return, you will receive remuneration for your time, acknowledgement in the research study and regular updates on early research findings.

We have also linked with our local Clinical Commissioning Group to access opinions from lay representatives during the preparation of this application. The chairperson of the CCG Patient and Public Engagement committee is a co-applicant. Under his leadership, we will establish a Service User Group that will contribute to all stages of the research.

About us

This research project is a joint collaboration between research teams from University of Surrey, Univeristy of Kent, Tangerine Bee, University of Leeds, and University of Exeter.

Principle Investigators and Co-applicants

Professor Heather Gage, University of Surrey, Chief Investigator working closely with team including:

  • experts in primary care organisation, effectiveness, workforce and human resources from health /social policy (Peckham), psychology (Marchand, Richards) and nursing (Spillsbury) perspectives, using qualitative and mixed methods
  • technical specialists in big data and statistics (Joy), health economics (Jordan) and knowledge mobilisation for policy and planning in health systems and clinical engagement (Chilvers)
  • representative of the NHS management and commissioning community (Fuller), a STP clinical lead / former CCG clinical chair and chief clinical officer, and practicing GP
  • Patient and Public Involvement (PPI) representative (Brady), with experience of public sector management and local PPI work
  • Gage (PI), is director of a portfolio of research within the Surrey Health Economics Centre, and has a track record of project management and health services research on workforce issues
  • Collaborator (Fitzpatrick), Farr Institute of Health Informatics Data Scientist, for liaison over data access
